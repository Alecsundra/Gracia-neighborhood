Streets and Squeres

If you just feel like walking, Gracia is a wonderful place to do it. Streets full of charm in which that atmosphere of a people of all life lasts. In many of these streets you will be able to see fun traffic signs decorated with stickers. On Verdi Street, for example, you will find a variety of restaurants with typical food from practically all countries. And if you want to change and visit the district of L’Eixample, you can walk along the Paseo de Sant Joan, whose last section, which connects both areas of Barcelona, was inaugurated in 1929. Squares, buildings, streets and parks. The Gracia neighborhood in Barcelona is a meeting place where culture, tradition and art come together to offer you an unforgettable day touring one of the most iconic districts of the city.

The Smallest Theater in the World

The Gracia neighborhood boasts the honor of hosting the smallest theater in the world. It is located on Encarnación street number 25 and is located on the ground floor of an old farmhouse from 1890. It was opened to the public in 1999 and concerts, shows and events are held here, all on a magical stage and under an atmosphere Familiar and cozy.
